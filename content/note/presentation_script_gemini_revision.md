# Presentation Script

## Introduction
- Hello everyone, my name is Chu Quang Lượng. Today I want to talk to you about...
(ad show).
- Oh, it seems we have a technical issue here, let me fix it real quick.
(closes ad)
- Okay. So you see, advertisements, or ads, are annoying, right? They distract you from the site's content and redirect you to other websites. Some ads even track users' behaviour. [^1]
- But what if I told you, there's a piece of technology that can get rid of all the advertisements you see on websites? Introducing... ad blockers.

## What are ad blockers? How do they work?
- An ad blocker is a piece of software that automatically hide/block advertisements from showing on websites or applications. The most common form of an ad blocker is through browser extensions such as AdBlock, uBlock Origin, etc.
- So, how do ad blockers work? When you access a website, the web browser makes HTTP requests to the web servers that host the website. The web servers usually return the website's content to the web browser for display.
- To load ads in the website's content, the web browser makes requests to ad servers. This is where our ad blockers come into play. Ad blockers use a list that contains several rules that identify which URLs point to an ad server, and block requests to them. [^2]
- Sometimes ads can be sneakily shown without triggering an HTTP request by encoding images in base64. Ad blockers also contain a list of CSS element selectors that find ad elements and hide them.

## Benefits of ad blockers
- Ad blockers help reduce distractions on the web, helps user to focus on the site's content. Ad blockers also help increase user engagement and activity on the site. [^3]
- Ad blockers can reduce traffic to the site, thus improving site's performance and reducing a device's power usage. [^4]

## Response from websites
- Despite having many benefits for users, ad blockers can reduce a website's revenue through ads. According to a report, in 2024, ad blocking solutions cost publishers $54 billion in lost ad revenue [^5]. So it's natural for website owners to implement anti-ad-blocker features.
- One of the ways these features are implemented is by blocking content if an ad blocker is detected. A pop-up might appear asking you to disable or whitelist the current site to maintain access to the site's content.

## Solutions/Alternatives
- While using ad blockers provides many benefits, you should consider whitelisting some sites to support site owners. Some platform also provide ad-free subscription to their services.
- If you're a website owner/creator, you also have a lot of options to recover from lost ad-blocking revenue. You can embed ads directly in your content, which might not be detected by ad blockers. Making ads less disruptive and more "visually appealing" can also persuade users to disable ad blocking on your site.

## Conclusion
- In this presentation, I've shown you how ad blockers work, the benefits and drawbacks of ad blockers and some tips for both users and creators. Hope this helps you to use the Internet more safely, easily and with less annoyance. Thank you for listening.
